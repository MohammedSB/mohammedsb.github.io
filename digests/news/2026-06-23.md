## Top Stories

### 1. [Iran agrees to UN nuclear inspectors’ return as part of agreement with US](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/jun/22/iran-us-talks-progress-pakistan-qatar-lebanon-israel)
*The Guardian · UK · left-leaning*

**What happened:** US Vice-President JD Vance announced that Iran agreed to allow UN nuclear inspectors back into the country, in exchange for Washington lifting sanctions on Iranian oil exports and reopening the Strait of Hormuz, following talks in Switzerland. Iran's foreign ministry pushed back, saying it made 'no new commitments' and that there are no plans to let the IAEA inspect sites bombed by the US and Israel last year.

**Background:** The talks follow a roughly four-month US-Israel war on Iran and an agreement to reach a final deal within 60 days; the US issued a 60-day license permitting Iranian oil transactions.

**Why it matters:** A durable US-Iran agreement would reshape regional security, energy markets (Hormuz carries a large share of global oil/LNG), and the balance of power between Iran and Gulf/Israeli interests.

**Different views:** The Jerusalem Post and BBC foreground the contradiction — Trump/Vance trumpet inspections and a 'major milestone' while Tehran and IRGC-linked Tasnim deny any inspection deal; the Guardian frames Trump's victory claims skeptically as covering a 'self-made catastrophe,' while Israeli commentators cast the document as provisional and warn it boosts Tehran.


### 2. [UN commission of inquiry says Israel committing genocide in Gaza by deliberately targeting children](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c0jy96w6pw2o?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)
*BBC Middle East · UK · mainstream*

**What happened:** A three-member UN Commission of Inquiry concluded that Israel is committing genocide in Gaza, citing the deliberate targeting of Palestinian children. Israel rejected the report as a 'libellous sham.'

**Background:** The finding adds to a body of UN and international legal scrutiny of Israel's conduct in Gaza during the ongoing war and its aftermath.

**Why it matters:** A formal UN genocide determination intensifies legal and diplomatic pressure on Israel and its allies and shapes international debate over accountability.

**Different views:** Al Jazeera presents the genocide finding as established fact in its headlines ('targeted in genocide'); the BBC attributes the claim to the panel and prominently includes Israel's 'libellous sham' rejection; the Jerusalem Post largely sidesteps the inquiry and instead runs material questioning Gaza casualty figures (e.g. alleged sampling flaws in the Lancet mortality survey).


### 3. [Israeli fire kills two in Lebanon as Hezbollah slams truce ‘violation’](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/23/israeli-fire-kills-two-in-lebanon-as-hezbollah-slams-truce-violation?traffic_source=rss)
*Al Jazeera English · Qatar · pro-Palestinian*

**What happened:** Israeli fire killed two people in Lebanon, prompting Hezbollah to denounce a 'treacherous' and 'blatant' violation of the ceasefire. Qatar's PM called it 'unacceptable' that Israel had killed nearly 100 Lebanese in a few days.

**Background:** A fragile ceasefire governs Israel-Hezbollah hostilities, and Iran has made a Lebanon ceasefire a condition in its US negotiations; talks involving Israeli and Lebanese officials are slated for Washington.

**Why it matters:** Continued strikes threaten to unravel the truce and could derail the broader US-Iran diplomacy, while raising questions about Hezbollah's disarmament and Lebanese state sovereignty.

**Different views:** Al Jazeera and Qatar's PM frame the strikes as Israeli aggression and ceasefire violations; the Jerusalem Post frames the same events through Israel's security lens — debating IDF 'slow war' doctrine, leaving Hezbollah intact near the border, and proposals to hand compounds to the Lebanese army as a test of its will to confront Hezbollah.
