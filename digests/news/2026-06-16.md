### 1. [US and Iran agree deal to end war as Trump says Strait of Hormuz to reopen](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c39yvvy273ko?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)
*BBC Middle East*

**What happened:** The US and Iran agreed a deal declaring the 'immediate and permanent termination of military operations on all fronts, including in Lebanon,' according to Pakistan, which helped broker it. President Trump announced the key Strait of Hormuz waterway would reopen, and both sides moved toward a formal signing later in the week.

**Background:** The agreement caps a roughly 15-week war that began when US and Israeli forces attacked Iran in late February, killing thousands, mostly in Iran and Lebanon, and choking global energy supplies.

**Why it matters:** It is the central regional development, ending active fighting and reopening Hormuz, but its vague terms leave Iran's nuclear program, Israel's role, and Lebanon's status unresolved.


### 2. [Netanyahu declares victory over Iran – and rules out withdrawal from Lebanon](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/jun/15/israel-territory-lebanon-defence-minister-israel-katz)
*The Guardian*

**What happened:** Benjamin Netanyahu hailed a 'historic victory' over Iran but ruled out any immediate Israeli withdrawal from Lebanon, saying forces would remain 'for as long as necessary.' His stance fueled anger inside Israel and criticism that he is undercutting the US-Iran framework.

**Background:** The preliminary US-Iran agreement was framed as ending fighting on all fronts including Lebanon, putting Netanyahu at odds with Trump, who publicly urged him to be 'more responsible' there.

**Why it matters:** Israel's refusal to pull out of Lebanon directly threatens the durability of the ceasefire and exposes a widening rift between Netanyahu and Washington.


### 3. [Families return to shattered towns as fragile ceasefire holds in Lebanon](https://www.aljazeera.com/gallery/2026/6/16/families-return-to-shattered-towns-as-fragile-ceasefire-holds-in-lebanon?traffic_source=rss)
*Al Jazeera English*

**What happened:** Displaced families began returning to shattered towns in southern Lebanon as a fragile ceasefire held, finding destroyed homes and dire conditions. Their return remains shadowed by uncertainty over whether the truce will last.

**Background:** Southern Lebanon was a major front in the US-Israel war against Iran, and the new US-Iran deal claims to cover Lebanon, though Israeli forces remain on the ground.

**Why it matters:** The human cost and precarious return illustrate whether the diplomatic deal translates into real stability or collapses back into conflict.
