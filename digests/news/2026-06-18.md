## Top Stories

### 1. [Trump signs Iran peace plan, claiming deal averts ‘worldwide depression’](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/jun/17/trump-us-iran-war-mou-deal)
*The Guardian*

**What happened:** Donald Trump signed a 14-point memorandum of understanding with Iran at the Palace of Versailles, ending the 110-day war and claiming it averted a 'worldwide depression.' The deal reopens the Strait of Hormuz and includes commitments that Iran will never develop a nuclear weapon, though officials acknowledge significant concessions to Tehran and the nuclear program's fate is still to be negotiated.

**Background:** The agreement caps a costly conflict the US launched with maximalist goals of dismantling Iran's nuclear and ballistic missile programs and ending its regional proxy support.

**Why it matters:** It marks a major de-escalation of a war that destabilized the region and global economy, but leaves core nuclear and sanctions questions unresolved, setting up future friction.


### 2. [Israel launches fresh strikes on Lebanon despite Trump criticism](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c7vyn17g832o?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)
*BBC Middle East*

**What happened:** Israel launched fresh strikes on Lebanon despite public criticism from Trump, who said Netanyahu needed to 'be more responsible with respect to Lebanon' and to 'stop blowing up buildings.' The strikes came as the US-Iran ceasefire framework took effect.

**Background:** The Lebanon file was a sticking point in the US-Iran MoU, and analysts argue real change requires Washington to force Israel's hand.

**Why it matters:** The continued strikes expose tensions between the US and Israel just as a regional peace framework is being implemented, threatening the durability of the ceasefire.


### 3. [Netanyahu under pressure in Israel after US-Iran agreement](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/newsfeed/2026/6/18/netanyahu-under-pressure-in-israel-after-us-iran-agreement?traffic_source=rss)
*Al Jazeera English*

**What happened:** Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu is facing mounting domestic pressure after being cut out of the US-Iran agreement negotiated directly between Washington and Tehran.

**Background:** Israel had been a central actor in the conflict with Iran but was sidelined in the final diplomacy, a deal not widely welcomed in Israel.

**Why it matters:** Netanyahu's exclusion signals a possible shift in US-Israel dynamics and could have major consequences for his political survival and Israeli policy in the region.
