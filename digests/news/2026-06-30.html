1. US says it has agreed to 'stand down' after exchange of strikes with Iran

BBC Middle East · UK · mainstream

What happened: The US and Iran exchanged fresh strikes over the weekend, with each accusing the other of violating the ceasefire, before Washington said it had agreed to 'stand down.' Trump claimed Iran agreed to hold talks in Doha, though the dispute centers on the strait of Hormuz and frozen Iranian assets.

Background: The clashes follow a US-Iran war and a fragile interim memorandum of understanding meant to keep the strait of Hormuz open and release roughly $12bn in frozen Iranian funds.

Why it matters: A collapse of the ceasefire risks reigniting a US-Iran war and closing a vital global shipping chokepoint, with major energy and geopolitical consequences.

Different views: Al Jazeera frames Tehran as sending a delegation only over frozen funds and questions whether direct talks exist; the Jerusalem Post stresses no direct talks and that Iran wins sanctions relief without curbing its nuclear/missile programs; Guardian emphasizes Iran's leverage over Hormuz and US escalation threats.

2. Israel-Lebanon framework faces first test as Hezbollah rejects disarmament deal - analysis

The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning

What happened: A US-brokered Israel-Lebanon framework aiming to establish diplomatic ties faces its first major test as Hezbollah flatly rejects disarmament and a senior Lebanese official insists the deal 'won't be implemented.' Analysts question whether Lebanon's government can enforce state authority in the south.

Background: The framework, negotiated in Washington, seeks to move from ceasefire to normalization between Israel and Lebanon, conditioned on Hezbollah disarmament and Lebanese sovereignty.

Why it matters: Success would be a historic Arab-Israeli normalization, but Hezbollah's rejection could entrench the stalemate or reignite conflict on Israel's northern border.

Different views: The Jerusalem Post presents it as a stability step backed by Gulf states (UAE's Gargash) while flagging Hezbollah's 'surrender' rejection; coverage centers on whether Beirut can enforce disarmament, with Hezbollah/Iran-aligned actors casting it as capitulation to Israel.
