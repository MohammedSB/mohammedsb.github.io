### 1. [US-Iran negotiations continue without high-level talks, Qatari Foreign Ministry says](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/article-901014)
*The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning*

**What happened:** US and Iranian delegations are in Doha for indirect, Qatari-mediated negotiations over an interim nuclear deal, but no direct or high-level talks are taking place. US envoys Witkoff and Kushner met Qatar's PM to lay groundwork for technical sessions while a ceasefire and MoU remain in place but unenforced, with frozen Iranian funds not yet released.

**Background:** The talks follow a recent US-Israeli war against Iran and prior Switzerland talks; Iran retains over 400kg of 60%-enriched uranium despite a damaged nuclear program.

**Why it matters:** The outcome will determine whether the fragile post-war ceasefire holds or collapses back into open conflict, and shapes Iran's nuclear trajectory and regional stability.

**Different views:** Al Jazeera frames it as active US-Iran negotiations mediated by Qatar; BBC stresses no direct US-Iran contact is scheduled; the Jerusalem Post foregrounds Iranian internal rifts (Pezeshkian vs IRGC), Iran's Hormuz toll demands, and residual nuclear threats, casting Iran as the untrustworthy party.

### 3. [Israel-Lebanon deal may entrench stalemate rather than end war - analysis](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/article-900908)
*The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning*

**What happened:** Analysts assess the recent Israel-Lebanon agreement, built on a bargain in which Hezbollah has flatly rejected disarmament and no Lebanese government has the power to enforce it. Commentators warn the deal may entrench a stalemate or even set the stage for a future war rather than end the conflict.

**Background:** The agreement follows Israel's war against Hezbollah and repeated cross-border escalation in southern Lebanon.

**Why it matters:** A deal that cannot be enforced risks collapsing into renewed conflict, with implications for Lebanon's sovereignty and regional escalation.

**Different views:** Al Jazeera argues the agreement paves the way for the next war and is designed so Lebanon will be blamed for it; the Jerusalem Post frames the unworkable core as Hezbollah's refusal to disarm, and US envoy Huckabee calls Hezbollah 'Lebanon's true enemy.'
