## Top Stories

### 1. [Iran says it has closed Strait of Hormuz over Israeli attacks in Lebanon](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwyekkwm1mmo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)
*BBC Middle East · UK · mainstream*

**What happened:** Iran announced it had closed the Strait of Hormuz in retaliation for Israeli attacks on Lebanon, with the IRGC warning ships away from the waterway. The US military publicly disputed Tehran's claim that the strait was actually closed, and the move came just ahead of scheduled US-Iran talks.

**Background:** The closure threat follows a recently signed US-Iran memorandum of understanding meant to end their conflict; Hormuz carries a large share of global seaborne oil.

**Why it matters:** Closing or mining the strait threatens global energy supplies and could unravel the fragile interim US-Iran deal, raising the risk of renewed wider war.

**Different views:** BBC and Jerusalem Post stress that the US military disputes the closure claim, framing it as possibly exaggerated Iranian posturing, while Al Jazeera presents it as a direct, justified response to Israeli aggression and the Guardian/Jerusalem Post focus on the physical reality of ~80 mines blocking shipping.


### 2. [Lebanon says Israeli strikes kill 47 as Israel says four soldiers killed by Hezbollah](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c23ymz1n9rmo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)
*BBC Middle East · UK · mainstream*

**What happened:** Lebanon said Israeli strikes killed dozens of people (reported figures of 47 and later tolls) in one of the deadliest days since March, while Israel said four of its soldiers were killed by Hezbollah fire. A ceasefire was announced and then renewed even as both sides continued strikes, and the flareup disrupted the opening of US-Iran talks.

**Background:** The violence erupted a day after the US and Iran signed a deal meant to end their conflict, including the fighting in Lebanon.

**Why it matters:** Continued Israel-Hezbollah combat threatens to collapse both the Lebanon ceasefire and the broader US-Iran agreement, and US intelligence reportedly warns Israeli operations could undermine the MoU.

**Different views:** Al Jazeera and the Guardian foreground Lebanese civilian deaths and Israeli occupation/destruction of villages, while the Jerusalem Post emphasizes Hezbollah drone attacks, dead Israeli soldiers, and frames the 80 strikes as a proportionate response; BBC reports both tolls side by side.


### 3. [Witkoff, Araghchi traveling to Switzerland to begin US-Iran nuclear talks - report](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/article-899959)
*The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning*

**What happened:** US envoy Steve Witkoff and Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi traveled to Switzerland to begin US-Iran nuclear talks, with JD Vance emerging as a central figure. The talks were briefly postponed/paused amid the Hormuz closure and Lebanon strikes before being set to begin Sunday, mediated by Pakistan and Qatar.

**Background:** The negotiations follow a memorandum of understanding ending the 110-day war, with reported plans to unfreeze billions in Iranian funds for humanitarian purchases.

**Why it matters:** The outcome will determine whether Iran's nuclear program is verifiably constrained and whether sanctions relief and regional de-escalation hold.

**Different views:** Al Jazeera frames the talks around US obligations and travel/sanctions grievances against Iran, the Jerusalem Post emphasizes Vance's isolationism, IRGC business gains from sanctions relief, and Israeli sense of betrayal, while the Guardian probes what authoritarian lessons Iran's leadership may draw.
