### 1. [US and Iran exchange strikes and accuse each other of violating ceasefire](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cdxdwkgqgq0o?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)
*BBC Middle East · UK · mainstream*

**What happened:** The US and Iran exchanged fresh strikes over the weekend, each accusing the other of violating their recently signed ceasefire. Iran says it launched retaliatory missile and drone attacks on US infrastructure in Kuwait and Bahrain after US forces hit multiple targets across Iran, before both sides agreed to 'stand down' and return to talks in Doha.

**Background:** The exchange came roughly 10 days after Iran and the US signed a memorandum of understanding to end their conflict, a document now appearing too vaguely worded over Lebanon and the Strait of Hormuz.

**Why it matters:** The flare-up threatens to unravel a fragile interim peace deal and drag the two countries back toward open war, while disrupting Hormuz shipping and pushing oil prices up.

**Different views:** BBC frames it neutrally as a mutual ceasefire violation; the Guardian emphasizes Trump's threats to 'annihilate' Iran and faults the memorandum's loose drafting; Al Jazeera highlights the Hormuz/oil disruption and a possible Trump-team split, while the Jerusalem Post stresses Iran's aggression, Bahrain's UN appeal, and details of CENTCOM's newly-built Iranian targets.


### 2. [Israel-Lebanon framework faces first test as Hezbollah rejects disarmament deal - analysis](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/article-900787)
*The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning*

**What happened:** A US-brokered trilateral framework between Israel and Lebanon was published, aiming to end decades of conflict and establish diplomatic relations, but faces its first major test as Hezbollah rejects disarmament and a senior Lebanese official insists the deal 'won't be implemented.' Hezbollah supporters rioted and the group's chief called it a humiliating surrender.

**Background:** The deal follows Israel's recent operations against Hezbollah and a reduced security zone in southern Lebanon; Gulf states including the UAE have cautiously welcomed it as restoring Lebanese sovereignty.

**Why it matters:** Lebanon's ability to enforce state authority over Hezbollah in the south is the central question determining whether a 78-year conflict can actually end or collapses back into violence.

**Different views:** The Jerusalem Post presents the framework as a historic peace step and stability win, foregrounding Hezbollah's intransigence as the obstacle; Al Jazeera frames continued Israeli actions (e.g. the southern Lebanon explosion) and Lebanese sovereignty concerns more critically, emphasizing the deal's rejection on the ground.


### 3. [Leaked draft reveals Trump's Gaza Board of Peace seeks total legal immunity, free property - report](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/article-900662)
*The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning*

**What happened:** A leaked draft of Trump's proposed Gaza 'Board of Peace' reveals it would grant all members total legal immunity from arrest, detention or legal proceedings in Gaza, along with property rights. This comes as Israeli analysis says Hamas retains full control of Gaza after deterring protests, including by publicizing executions of alleged collaborators.

**Background:** The Board of Peace is part of a postwar governance plan for Gaza amid an ongoing fragile ceasefire that pro-Palestinian outlets report Israel continues to violate with deadly strikes.

**Why it matters:** The immunity provisions raise serious questions about accountability and sovereignty in any postwar Gaza administration, while Hamas's enduring grip undermines the premise of externally-imposed governance.

**Different views:** The Jerusalem Post frames Hamas's continued control as evidence of its repression and the Board of Peace leak skeptically; Al Jazeera and the Guardian foreground continued Israeli strikes killing civilians and children and the broader humanitarian/accountability picture.
