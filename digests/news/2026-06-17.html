1. Middle East crisis live: Trump warns US will ‘go back to shooting’ if Iran ‘don’t behave’, as G7 leaders call for Lebanon ceasefire

The Guardian

What happened: Trump warned the US would 'go back to shooting' if Iran 'don't behave' and said the US-Iran deal is 'not final,' while denying it includes a $300bn reconstruction fund for Tehran. He backed a G7 statement calling for a Lebanon ceasefire and wider talks on Iran's ballistic missile programme, an issue not covered in the current MOU.

Background: The US and Iran reached a memorandum of understanding to end their war, set for a ceremonial signing Friday in Geneva, after a roughly 110-day conflict that drew in Lebanon and disrupted Gulf oil shipping.

Why it matters: The framework caps a major regional war but leaves missiles, nuclear limits, and the Lebanon front unresolved, meaning the ceasefire is fragile and could collapse back into open conflict.

2. Israel launches fresh strikes on Lebanon despite Trump criticism

BBC Middle East

What happened: Israel launched fresh airstrikes on Lebanon despite public criticism from Trump, who said Netanyahu needed 'to be more responsible with respect to Lebanon.' Trump separately suggested too many civilians are being killed and that Syria might do a better job than Israel of 'taking care of Hezbollah.'

Background: The strikes come as the newly negotiated US-Iran truce is being tested; Iran has warned that continued Israeli attacks on Lebanon threaten the broader deal and could trigger a 'harsh response.'

Why it matters: The strikes expose growing friction between Trump and Netanyahu and show how the Lebanon front could unravel the wider US-Iran agreement.

3. Israel takes control of Hebron’s Ibrahimi Mosque: What this means

Al Jazeera English

What happened: Israel took control of planning and construction at Hebron's Ibrahimi Mosque (Cave of the Patriarchs), overturning arrangements under which Palestinians had controlled such matters across the West Bank city.

Background: Under the 1997 Hebron Agreement, Palestinians held planning and construction authority in the city; the holy site is contested between Muslims and Jews and has long been a flashpoint.

Why it matters: The move further erodes Palestinian authority in the occupied West Bank and risks inflaming religious tensions at one of the region's most sensitive sites.
