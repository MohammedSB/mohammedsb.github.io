### 2. [US, Israel, Lebanon agreement is ‘first step to restoring Lebanon’s sovereignty,' Aoun says](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/article-900623)
*The Jerusalem Post · Israeli · right-leaning*

**What happened:** Lebanon, Israel, and the US signed a 14-point framework agreement, hailed by Lebanese President Aoun as a 'first step to restoring Lebanon's sovereignty,' built on a performance-based 'trust, but verify' system requiring Lebanon to rein in Hezbollah. Israel struck southern Lebanon a day later, killing at least one person, as Hezbollah chief Naim Qassem rejected the deal as a 'humiliation' and 'null.'

**Background:** The agreement attempts to formally wind down decades of Israel-Lebanon conflict, tying Israeli withdrawal from a southern security zone to Hezbollah's disarmament.

**Why it matters:** It could mark a historic end to a 78-year conflict, but Hezbollah's rejection, continued strikes, and unresolved accountability questions threaten its durability.

**Different views:** The Jerusalem Post casts it as a path to Lebanese sovereignty and a victory over Hezbollah; Al Jazeera and Hezbollah frame it as a humiliating concession undermining sovereignty; the Guardian warns it may block war-crimes victims from ICC justice against Israel.


### 3. [Trump’s Board of Peace plans to grant itself sweeping immunity, documents show](https://www.theguardian.com/law/2026/jun/27/board-of-peace-legal-immunity-un)
*The Guardian · UK · left-leaning*

**What happened:** A leaked draft resolution shows Trump's UN-sanctioned 'Board of Peace,' created to govern post-war Gaza, plans to grant itself and its security forces sweeping legal immunity from arrest, detention, or prosecution, and to acquire public property in Gaza.

**Background:** The Board of Peace was announced by Trump to administer Gaza following the October 2025 ceasefire, positioning an external body in control of the territory.

**Why it matters:** Self-granted immunity and property powers raise serious accountability, sovereignty, and rule-of-law concerns over how Gaza will be governed.

**Different views:** The Guardian frames the immunity grab critically as shielding members from prosecution; the Jerusalem Post reports the same leaked draft more neutrally as a factual disclosure of the immunities and property provisions.
