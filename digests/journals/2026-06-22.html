1. General-purpose chatbots outperform clinical AI tools on physicians’ real-world questions

Nature Medicine  ·  Jun 17, 2026

Clinical AILLM Evaluation

This Nature Medicine study found that general-purpose chatbots (like ChatGPT) answered physicians' real-world clinical questions better than purpose-built clinical AI tools. It raises important questions about how specialized medical AI systems should be benchmarked and whether foundation models are outpacing bespoke clinical software.

5. FetalCLIP: a visual-language foundation model for fetal ultrasound image analysis

npj Digital Medicine  ·  Jun 20, 2026

Foundation ModelsVision-Language ModelsMedical Imaging

FetalCLIP is a CLIP-style visual-language foundation model trained on fetal ultrasound image-text pairs for downstream analysis tasks. It directly extends CLIP-style contrastive pretraining into a specialized medical imaging domain, offering a transferable backbone for fetal ultrasound.

6. REN: Anatomically-Informed Mixture-of-Experts for Interstitial Lung Disease Diagnosis.

IEEE Trans. Medical Imaging  ·  Jun 17, 2026

Medical ImagingDeep Learning Architectures

REN is an anatomically-informed Mixture-of-Experts architecture for interstitial lung disease diagnosis that routes inputs based on anatomical structure rather than domain-agnostic gating. It tailors conditional computation to the regional heterogeneity of disease in medical images.

7. Myocardial Temporal-Mechanical Self-Supervision Model for Contrast-Free Myocardial Infarction Segmentation with Label-Free Training.

IEEE Trans. Medical Imaging  ·  Jun 17, 2026

Self-Supervised LearningMedical ImagingSegmentation

This work introduces a temporal-mechanical self-supervised model for contrast-free myocardial infarction segmentation that requires no labels and avoids reliance on paired contrast-enhanced images. It is a strong example of label-free self-supervision applied to a clinically valuable segmentation problem.

8. Artificial Intelligence for Digital Breast Tomosynthesis Screening with and without Prior Examinations in BreastScreen Norway.

This BreastScreen Norway study evaluated an AI model for digital breast tomosynthesis screening, with and without access to prior examinations, against double radiologist reading. It quantifies how incorporating prior imaging affects AI cancer-detection performance in a real screening program.
