### 1. [Generalised Medical Phrase Grounding.](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMI.2026.3707568)
*IEEE Trans. Medical Imaging · Jun 26, 2026*  
**Authors:** Wenjian Zhang, Shekhar S Chandra, Aaron Nicolson  
**Tags:** Referring Segmentation, Vision-Language Models, Radiology AI

This paper generalizes medical phrase grounding beyond the one-box-per-phrase assumption to handle multi-region radiological findings, mapping report text to the correct set of image regions. It directly targets the referring-expression-comprehension paradigm for radiology, making grounded reports more interpretable.

### 2. [Towards clinical-level interpretation of dental panoramic radiography using an instance-guided vision-language model](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41551-026-01713-8)
*Nature Biomedical Engineering · Jun 25, 2026*  
**Authors:** Qikui Zhu, Yeyu Lin, Weitao Fu, Wenhui Tang et al. (19 authors)  
**Tags:** Vision-Language Models, Medical Imaging, Foundation Models

An instance-guided vision-language model is proposed to produce clinical-level interpretation of dental panoramic radiographs, linking detected instances to textual descriptions. It demonstrates how VLM architectures can move toward expert-grade report-style outputs in a new imaging domain.

### 3. [A multimodal instruction dataset and benchmark for ultrasound understanding](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-026-02930-w)
*npj Digital Medicine · Jun 26, 2026*  
**Authors:** Shijie Wang, Yingnan Wu, Yilun Zhang, Zeyu Lai et al. (6 authors)  
**Tags:** Multimodal Learning, Medical Imaging, Benchmarking

The authors release a multimodal instruction dataset and benchmark for ultrasound image understanding, enabling training and evaluation of instruction-following vision-language models. It provides a standardized resource for assessing multimodal medical AI in a modality that has lacked such benchmarks.

### 4. [SUDA: Simultaneous unsupervised knowledge distillation and adaptation of foundation models for efficient pathological image analysis.](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.media.2026.104177)
*Medical Image Analysis · Jun 26, 2026*  
**Authors:** Lanfeng Zhong, Kun Qian, Weiren Zhao, Tian Shen et al. (7 authors)  
**Tags:** Foundation Models, Self-Supervised Learning, Computational Pathology

SUDA combines unsupervised knowledge distillation with domain adaptation to compress large pathology foundation models while correcting pretraining-to-downstream domain shift. It addresses the dual barriers of computational cost and distribution shift that limit clinical deployment of foundation models.

### 5. [Impact of Exposure Parameters on Deep Learning Models in Chest Radiography and Implications for Deployment.](https://doi.org/10.1148/ryai.250731)
*Radiology: AI · Jun 24, 2026*  
**Authors:** Han-Jay Shu, Shun-Ting Chang, Rodrigo Rosa Gameiro, Judy Wawira Gichoya et al. (6 authors)  
**Tags:** Chest X-ray, Radiology AI, Model Robustness

This study shows that deep learning models trained on chest radiographs exploit radiographic exposure parameters as shortcut features, introducing biases under confounded and natural exposure regimes. It has important implications for the safe deployment of CXR models in real clinical settings.

### 6. [From breadth to depth in clinical artificial intelligence evaluation](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41551-026-01691-x)
*Nature Biomedical Engineering · Jun 24, 2026*  
**Authors:** Liam G. McCoy, David Wu  
**Tags:** Clinical AI Evaluation, Foundation Models

A perspective arguing that clinical AI evaluation must shift from broad benchmark breadth toward deeper, more rigorous assessment of real-world readiness. It is directly relevant to thinking about evaluation methodology for medical and generative models.

### 7. [Evaluating the robustness and readiness of large frontier models in health AI applications](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04501-8)
*Nature Medicine · Jun 26, 2026*  
**Authors:** Yu Gu, Jingjing Fu, Xiaodong Liu, Jeya Maria Jose Valanarasu et al. (32 authors)  
**Tags:** Foundation Models, LLM Evaluation, Clinical AI

This work evaluates the robustness and clinical readiness of large frontier (foundation/LLM) models across health AI applications, probing failure modes under realistic conditions. It offers a systematic framework for stress-testing general-purpose models before medical use.

### 8. [Benchmarking of AI and Radiologists for Indeterminate Lung Nodule Malignancy Risk Estimation on Screening CT: The LUNA25 Challenge.](https://doi.org/10.1148/ryai.260179)
*Radiology: AI · Jun 24, 2026*  
**Authors:** Dré Peeters, Bogdan Obreja, Noa Antonissen, Zaigham Saghir et al. (20 authors)  
**Tags:** Radiology AI, Benchmarking, Clinical AI Evaluation

The LUNA25 challenge benchmarks AI systems against radiologists for malignancy risk estimation of indeterminate 5–15 mm lung nodules on low-dose screening CT under a transparent evaluation framework. It provides a rigorous, standardized comparison of AI versus human performance on a clinically important screening task.
