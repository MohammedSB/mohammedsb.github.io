### 1. [General-purpose chatbots outperform clinical AI tools on physicians’ real-world questions](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04457-9)
*Nature Medicine · Jun 17, 2026*  
**Tags:** Clinical AI, LLM Evaluation

This Nature Medicine study found that general-purpose chatbots (like ChatGPT) answered physicians' real-world clinical questions better than purpose-built clinical AI tools. It raises important questions about how specialized medical AI systems should be benchmarked and whether foundation models are outpacing bespoke clinical software.

### 2. [Towards autonomous medical artificial intelligence agents](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10675-5)
*Nature · Jun 17, 2026*  
**Tags:** Medical AI Agents, Clinical AI, Foundation Models

A perspective on building autonomous medical AI agents that can reason, plan, and act across clinical workflows rather than performing single isolated tasks. It outlines the technical and safety roadmap needed to move from narrow diagnostic models toward agentic systems in medicine.

### 3. [Development of a multimodal large language model for early warning and diagnosis of chronic ocular GVHD](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-026-02916-8)
*npj Digital Medicine · Jun 22, 2026*  
**Tags:** Multimodal Learning, Vision-Language Models, Clinical AI

Researchers developed a multimodal large language model that integrates imaging and clinical data for early warning and diagnosis of chronic ocular graft-versus-host disease. The work demonstrates how vision-language models can be specialized for early detection in a narrow but high-impact clinical setting.

### 4. [An expert-level vision-language model for multitask diagnostic morphology in clinical laboratories](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-026-02905-x)
*npj Digital Medicine · Jun 22, 2026*  
**Tags:** Vision-Language Models, Medical Imaging, Foundation Models

This paper presents an expert-level vision-language model for multitask diagnostic morphology in clinical laboratories, handling tasks like cell and tissue classification across modalities. It shows that a single VLM can approach specialist performance across diverse diagnostic morphology tasks.

### 5. [FetalCLIP: a visual-language foundation model for fetal ultrasound image analysis](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-026-02907-9)
*npj Digital Medicine · Jun 20, 2026*  
**Tags:** Foundation Models, Vision-Language Models, Medical Imaging

FetalCLIP is a CLIP-style visual-language foundation model trained on fetal ultrasound image-text pairs for downstream analysis tasks. It directly extends CLIP-style contrastive pretraining into a specialized medical imaging domain, offering a transferable backbone for fetal ultrasound.

### 6. [REN: Anatomically-Informed Mixture-of-Experts for Interstitial Lung Disease Diagnosis.](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMI.2026.3704035)
*IEEE Trans. Medical Imaging · Jun 17, 2026*  
**Tags:** Medical Imaging, Deep Learning Architectures

REN is an anatomically-informed Mixture-of-Experts architecture for interstitial lung disease diagnosis that routes inputs based on anatomical structure rather than domain-agnostic gating. It tailors conditional computation to the regional heterogeneity of disease in medical images.

### 7. [Myocardial Temporal-Mechanical Self-Supervision Model for Contrast-Free Myocardial Infarction Segmentation with Label-Free Training.](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMI.2026.3704460)
*IEEE Trans. Medical Imaging · Jun 17, 2026*  
**Tags:** Self-Supervised Learning, Medical Imaging, Segmentation

This work introduces a temporal-mechanical self-supervised model for contrast-free myocardial infarction segmentation that requires no labels and avoids reliance on paired contrast-enhanced images. It is a strong example of label-free self-supervision applied to a clinically valuable segmentation problem.

### 8. [Artificial Intelligence for Digital Breast Tomosynthesis Screening with and without Prior Examinations in BreastScreen Norway.](https://doi.org/10.1148/ryai.250988)
*Radiology: AI · Jul 2026*  
**Tags:** Radiology AI, Clinical AI, Medical Imaging

This BreastScreen Norway study evaluated an AI model for digital breast tomosynthesis screening, with and without access to prior examinations, against double radiologist reading. It quantifies how incorporating prior imaging affects AI cancer-detection performance in a real screening program.
