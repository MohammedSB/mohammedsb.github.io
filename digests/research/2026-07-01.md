## Top Medical AI Papers (Full Summary)

### 1. [Seeing Through Multiple Views: Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning via Selective Neurons for Consistent Radiology Report Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31099)
**Authors:** Yucheng Chen, Jinjing Zhu, Yang Yu, Yufei Shi et al. (10 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.31099`  
**Tags:** Radiology Report Generation, Chest X-ray, Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning

**TL;DR:** A parameter-efficient method that selects view-specific neurons to reconcile multi-view chest X-ray inputs for more consistent radiology report generation.

**Problem:** Existing radiology report generation (RRG) methods fuse multi-view X-ray features directly, ignoring clinical inconsistencies that arise when one model processes different views, which harms accuracy and clinical reliability.

**Method:** The authors propose a parameter-efficient fine-tuning approach that identifies and activates 'selective neurons' specialized for handling multiple X-ray views, rather than naive feature fusion. This view-aware selection preserves cross-view consistency while adapting a foundation model with minimal trainable parameters.

**Results:** The method improves both report quality and multi-view clinical consistency over direct feature-fusion baselines on RRG benchmarks.

**Why it matters:** Directly targets the reader's core interest in radiology report generation and chest X-ray analysis, with a practical PEFT recipe for multi-view clinical imaging.


### 2. [Token-Sparse Medical Multimodal Reasoning via Dual-Stream Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31599)
**Authors:** Kaitao Chen, Weiqian Zhao, Jiamin Wu, Qihao Zheng et al. (9 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.31599`  
**Tags:** Medical Vision-Language Models, Reinforcement Learning, Visual Grounding

**TL;DR:** A dual-stream RL framework prunes visual tokens outside clinically relevant regions to sharpen medical multimodal reasoning in VLMs.

**Problem:** Medical images offer extremely sparse visual evidence for clinical decisions, and standard RL-tuned vision-language models struggle to focus on the diagnostically important regions.

**Method:** The paper introduces a token-sparse dual-stream reinforcement learning framework that actively prunes visual tokens lying outside the grounding region, concentrating reasoning on informative medical evidence. The two streams jointly optimize grounding and reasoning within a unified RL objective.

**Results:** Pruning non-grounding visual tokens substantially improves medical reasoning performance compared to dense-token RL baselines.

**Why it matters:** Combines vision-language models, RL post-training, and visual grounding for medical imaging—squarely within the reader's multimodal medical-imaging and referring-segmentation interests.


### 3. [HyperVLP: Enhancing Hierarchical Surgical Video-Language Pre-training in Hyperbolic Space](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31245)
**Authors:** Yaojun Hu, Kun Yuan, Nassir Navab, Haochao Ying et al. (6 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.31245`  
**Tags:** Surgical Vision-Language Pretraining, Hierarchical Representation Learning, Foundation Models

**TL;DR:** HyperVLP pretrains surgical video-language representations in hyperbolic space to capture the multi-level hierarchy of surgical knowledge.

**Problem:** Surgical knowledge from lecture videos is inherently hierarchical—fine-grained action cues in narration, mid-level steps in headings, and global procedural context—yet standard vision-language pretraining in Euclidean space fails to model this structure.

**Method:** HyperVLP performs hierarchical surgical video-language pretraining in hyperbolic space, which naturally embeds tree-like hierarchies. It aligns visual features with multi-granularity textual signals (action, step, procedure) to transfer structured surgical knowledge into visual representations.

**Results:** The hyperbolic hierarchical alignment yields improved surgical video representations and downstream task performance over Euclidean vision-language baselines.

**Why it matters:** Advances self-supervised, multimodal foundation-model learning for medical imaging (surgical video), matching the reader's focus on CLIP-style vision-language models applied to medicine.


## Top General AI Papers (Full Summary)

### 1. [GEAR: Guided End-to-End AutoRegression for Image Synthesis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.32039)
**Authors:** Bin Lin, Zheyuan Liu, Chenguo Lin, Sixiang Chen et al. (11 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.32039`  
**Tags:** Autoregressive Image Generation, Tokenizer Learning, Generative Models

**TL;DR:** GEAR jointly trains a VQ tokenizer end-to-end with the generator so the tokenizer learns representations the generator finds easy to model.

**Problem:** Standard visual generative pipelines train a tokenizer for reconstruction, freeze it, then train a generator on its codes—leaving the tokenizer unaware of what is actually easy for the generator to learn.

**Method:** GEAR (Guided End-to-end AutoRegression) trains a vector-quantized tokenizer and the autoregressive generator jointly, letting generator feedback guide the tokenizer's latent/discrete representation. This couples reconstruction and generation objectives that were previously decoupled.

**Results:** The end-to-end guided training improves image synthesis quality relative to conventional frozen-tokenizer autoregressive pipelines.

**Why it matters:** Addresses a foundational representation-learning question in generative modeling that transfers to tokenizer/encoder design broadly relevant to the reader's multimodal and self-supervised work.


### 2. [AdaJEPA: An Adaptive Latent World Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.32026)
**Authors:** Ying Wang, Oumayma Bounou, Yann LeCun, Mengye Ren  
**arXiv:** `2606.32026`  
**Tags:** World Models, Test-Time Adaptation, Self-Supervised Learning

**TL;DR:** AdaJEPA is a latent world model that adapts at test time to remain accurate under distribution shift during planning.

**Problem:** Latent world models are typically frozen at inference, so when their predictions become inaccurate under test-time distribution shift, planning fails.

**Method:** AdaJEPA performs test-time adaptation of a JEPA-style latent world model, updating the model online so its latent forward predictions stay reliable as observations drift. It integrates adaptation into the planning loop.

**Results:** The adaptive world model maintains planning accuracy under distribution shift where frozen latent world models degrade.

**Why it matters:** From a LeCun-led group, this advances self-supervised joint-embedding predictive architectures—directly connected to the reader's interest in self-supervised representation learning.


### 3. [Bridging the Gap Between Latent and Explicit Reasoning with Looped Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31779)
**Authors:** Ying Fan, Anej Svete, Kangwook Lee  
**arXiv:** `2606.31779`  
**Tags:** LLM Reasoning, Latent Chain-of-Thought, Looped Transformers

**TL;DR:** Looped transformers are used to close the performance gap between efficient latent reasoning and explicit token-by-token chain-of-thought.

**Problem:** Latent chain-of-thought reasons in hidden states for efficiency but underperforms explicit CoT beyond 1B parameters, with the gap widening at scale.

**Method:** The authors bridge latent and explicit reasoning using looped transformers, which iterate computation in latent space to emulate multi-step reasoning while retaining efficiency. They analyze how looping recovers the expressivity lost by decoding-free latent reasoning.

**Results:** The looped-transformer approach narrows the latent-vs-explicit reasoning gap at larger model scales.

**Why it matters:** A general architecture/training insight into reasoning models that informs efficient multimodal reasoning relevant to the reader's broader ML interests.
