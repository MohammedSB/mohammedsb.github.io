## Top Medical AI Papers (Full Summary)

### 1. [CADRE: Stable, Parameter Efficient Adaptation of Medical Vision Language Models with Bounded Forgetting and Prior Drift](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23487)
**Authors:** Amrita Singh, Rishabh Jha  
**arXiv:** `2606.23487`  
**Tags:** Medical Vision-Language Models, Parameter-Efficient Adaptation, Continual Learning

**TL;DR:** CADRE adapts medical VLMs (e.g. BiomedCLIP) to new clinical imaging services with bounded catastrophic forgetting and bounded drift from the trustworthy pretrained prior.

**Problem:** Updating a deployed medical VLM for a new imaging modality can silently harm patients by forgetting previously handled modalities or drifting away from the safe pretrained prior. Adaptation is therefore as much a safety problem as an accuracy problem.

**Method:** CADRE is a parameter-efficient adaptation method that explicitly constrains how far the model can move during fine-tuning, providing formal bounds on forgetting of prior modalities and on prior drift. It treats safe adaptation as a constrained optimization over the pretrained representation.

**Results:** The framework stably adapts BiomedCLIP-style models to new modalities while preserving prior-task performance and staying close to the original prior.

**Why it matters:** Directly relevant to building and safely deploying CLIP-style vision-language foundation models in clinical radiology workflows, a core interest of the reader.


### 2. [Evaluating self-supervised echocardiographic representations across downstream extraction strategies for left-ventricular segmentation and ejection fraction estimation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22943)
**Authors:** Sylwia Majchrowska, Philip Teare  
**arXiv:** `2606.22943`  
**Tags:** Self-Supervised Learning, Echocardiography, Cardiac Segmentation

**TL;DR:** A systematic study showing that judging self-supervised echocardiographic representations by a single downstream setup conflates representation quality with the capacity of the readout model.

**Problem:** SSL is increasingly used in medical imaging to cut annotation cost, but representation quality is usually evaluated with one downstream protocol, which can confound the encoder's quality with the strength of the head used to extract task-relevant signal.

**Method:** The authors evaluate self-supervised echocardiography encoders across multiple downstream extraction strategies (varying probe/decoder capacity) on left-ventricular segmentation and ejection-fraction estimation. They compare how rankings of SSL methods change as the readout capacity changes.

**Results:** Conclusions about which SSL method is best shift depending on the downstream extraction strategy, exposing that single-setting evaluation is unreliable for dense cardiac tasks.

**Why it matters:** Speaks directly to the reader's work on self-supervised learning for medical imaging and to how SSL representations should be evaluated, not just trained.


### 3. [Brain-Adapter: A Dual-Stream Vision-Language MIL Framework for Comprehensive 3D CT Diagnosis of Acute Intracranial Pathologies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23494)
**Authors:** Zhenyu Yi, Zhiyun Song, Yusong Sun, Zelin Liu et al. (9 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.23494`  
**Tags:** Medical Vision-Language Models, 3D CT Diagnosis, Multiple Instance Learning

**TL;DR:** Brain-Adapter transfers 2D vision-language foundation models to 3D brain CT for diagnosing acute intracranial pathologies via a dual-stream MIL framework.

**Problem:** Automated 3D brain CT diagnosis is critical for critical care but relies heavily on manual annotation, and conventional models have limited semantic understanding. Transferring powerful 2D VLMs to 3D volumes remains unsolved.

**Method:** Brain-Adapter is a dual-stream vision-language multiple-instance-learning framework that adapts pretrained 2D VLMs to 3D CT, treating slices/regions as instances and aligning visual features with textual semantics for comprehensive diagnosis.

**Results:** The method achieves strong, comprehensive diagnosis of acute intracranial pathologies on 3D CT while reducing reliance on dense manual annotations.

**Why it matters:** Combines vision-language foundation models with 3D medical imaging diagnosis, intersecting the reader's interests in multimodal learning and radiology AI.


## Top General AI Papers (Full Summary)

### 1. [Randomized YaRN Improves Length Generalization for Long-Context Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23687)
**Authors:** Manas Mehta, Fangcong Yin, Greg Durrett  
**arXiv:** `2606.23687`  
**Tags:** Long-Context LLMs, Positional Encoding, Length Generalization

**TL;DR:** Randomized YaRN combines YaRN positional extrapolation with randomized positional encoding to improve LLM generalization to very long sequences.

**Problem:** LLMs pretrained on short sequences and extended for longer contexts still fail to generalize to very long sequences during reasoning. Existing extension methods plateau.

**Method:** Randomized YaRN is a training method that fuses YaRN-based positional extrapolation with randomized positional encoding and a learned schedule, exposing the model to a wider range of effective positions during training.

**Results:** The approach improves length generalization for long-context reasoning beyond standard YaRN-extended baselines.

**Why it matters:** Long-context handling is foundational for multimodal and document-heavy models, including report generation pipelines that the reader works with.


### 2. [Provable Benefits of RLVR over SFT for Reasoning Models: Learning to Backtrack Efficiently](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22938)
**Authors:** Stanley Wei, Juno Kim  
**arXiv:** `2606.22938`  
**Tags:** Reinforcement Learning, LLM Reasoning, Learning Theory

**TL;DR:** A theoretical account of why reinforcement fine-tuning (RLVR) yields better reasoning than supervised fine-tuning, framed as learning to backtrack efficiently.

**Problem:** RLVR empirically improves LLM reasoning over SFT, but the theoretical reason for this advantage has been unclear.

**Method:** The authors model chain-of-thought reasoning as path search and analyze the training dynamics, showing that reinforcement fine-tuning induces efficient backtracking behavior that purely supervised methods cannot acquire.

**Results:** They prove a provable separation: RLVR learns to backtrack efficiently and reach correct reasoning paths where SFT fails, explaining the empirical reasoning gains.

**Why it matters:** Clarifies a key mechanism behind reasoning-model training, useful for understanding and applying RL-based post-training in vision-language reasoning systems.


### 3. [Visual Geometry Transformer in the Wild: Distractor-Free 3D Reconstruction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22787)
**Authors:** Tianbo Pan, Xingyi Yang, Shizun Wang, Xinchao Wang  
**arXiv:** `2606.22787`  
**Tags:** 3D Reconstruction, Vision Foundation Models, Robust Perception

**TL;DR:** A visual geometry transformer that performs distractor-free end-to-end multi-view 3D reconstruction robustly in real-world, non-static scenes.

**Problem:** End-to-end multi-view 3D reconstruction methods assume distractor-free static scenes with perfect cross-view geometry, so they fail in the wild where transient distractors and occlusions appear.

**Method:** The method extends a feed-forward visual geometry transformer to identify and discount transient distractors and occluded regions, enabling robust reconstruction without the idealized static-scene assumption.

**Results:** It achieves reliable 3D reconstruction in real-world settings where prior state-of-the-art end-to-end methods break down.

**Why it matters:** Robust feed-forward geometric foundation models are increasingly relevant to medical and general multi-view imaging where clean inputs are rare.
