1. Scalable Training of Spatially Grounded 2D Vision-Language Models for Radiology

Yusuf Salcan, Simon Ging, Robin Schirrmeister, Philipp Arnold et al. (7 authors)  ·  2606.20477

TL;DR: RefRad2D trains spatially grounded radiology vision-language models on 1.2M CT/MR image-text pairs without any manual spatial annotations.

Problem: Visually grounded radiology VLMs normally require costly manual spatial annotations linking text findings to image regions, which limits scalability.

Method: The authors build a large-scale bilingual (German/English) dataset of CT/MR image-text pairs with task-specific VQA and spatial-grounding subsets, generated automatically via LLM-based curation and automated segmentation. A 2D VLM is trained on these to produce both answers and spatial grounding.

Results: The model achieves visually grounded radiology understanding (VQA + grounding) without human spatial labels, validated across the curated subsets.

Why it matters: Directly central to the reader's interests in radiology VLMs, referring/spatial grounding, and scalable annotation-free training for medical imaging.

2. Confidence Calibration for Multimodal LLMs: An Empirical Study through Medical VQA

Yuetian Du, Yucheng Wang, Ming Kong, Tian Liang et al. (7 authors)  ·  2606.19950

TL;DR: A first comprehensive study of confidence calibration in medical multimodal LLMs via medical VQA, with a multi-strategy fusion method to align confidence with accuracy.

Problem: Medical MLLMs often express confidence that is misaligned with their actual accuracy, risking misdiagnosis or dismissal of correct advice.

Method: The work systematically analyzes the accuracy-confidence relationship across medical MLLMs and proposes a method combining multi-strategy fusion to better calibrate elicited confidence on medical VQA tasks.

Results: The proposed approach improves alignment between confidence and accuracy compared to baseline elicitation, demonstrated empirically on medical VQA.

Why it matters: Trustworthy/calibrated vision-language models for medicine bear directly on deploying generative and VQA systems in radiology and clinical settings.

3. OTCHA: Optimal Transport-driven Confidence-aware Latent Hub Alignment for Multi-View Medical Image Classification

Jiwoong Yang, Haejun Chung, Ikbeom Jang  ·  2606.19838

TL;DR: OTCHA uses optimal-transport-driven, confidence-aware latent hub alignment to fuse unregistered multi-view medical images (mammography, chest radiography) for classification.

Problem: Multi-view medical images are often unregistered and carry view-specific artifacts and irrelevant background, so naive fusion lets noise contaminate the joint embedding.

Method: OTCHA aligns per-view representations through a confidence-aware latent hub using optimal transport, suppressing irrelevant content before fusion rather than directly concatenating view features.

Results: The method improves multi-view classification by producing cleaner fused embeddings on standard multi-view modalities including chest radiography and mammography.

Why it matters: Multi-view fusion for chest radiography is squarely within the reader's focus on chest X-ray analysis and multimodal medical imaging.
