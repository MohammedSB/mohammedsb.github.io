## Top Medical AI Papers (Full Summary)

### 1. [Scalable Training of Spatially Grounded 2D Vision-Language Models for Radiology](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20477)
**Authors:** Yusuf Salcan, Simon Ging, Robin Schirrmeister, Philipp Arnold et al. (7 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.20477`  
**Tags:** Radiology Vision-Language Models, Spatial Grounding, Medical Imaging

**TL;DR:** A method to train spatially grounded 2D vision-language models for CT/MR radiology without manual annotations, using LLM-curated VQA and grounding data.

**Problem:** Radiology VLMs typically lack spatial grounding, and obtaining manual spatial annotations at scale is prohibitively expensive.

**Method:** The authors build RefRad2D, a 1.2M bilingual (German/English) CT/MR image-text dataset with VQA and spatial grounding subsets generated automatically via LLM-based curation and automated segmentation. They train a VLM on this data to produce visually grounded radiology outputs without manual spatial labels.

**Results:** The trained model achieves grounded radiology understanding (VQA + localization) without requiring manual spatial annotation, demonstrating annotation-free scalability.

**Why it matters:** Directly central to the reader's interests in vision-language foundation models for radiology and grounded report generation, with a scalable annotation-free recipe.


### 2. [Confidence Calibration for Multimodal LLMs: An Empirical Study through Medical VQA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19950)
**Authors:** Yuetian Du, Yucheng Wang, Ming Kong, Tian Liang et al. (7 authors)  
**arXiv:** `2606.19950`  
**Tags:** Medical VQA, Multimodal LLMs, Confidence Calibration

**TL;DR:** A comprehensive study of the accuracy-confidence mismatch in medical multimodal LLMs, proposing a fusion method to better calibrate elicited confidence.

**Problem:** Medical MLLMs often express confidence that misaligns with their actual accuracy, risking misdiagnosis or dismissal of correct advice.

**Method:** The work performs the first comprehensive analysis of the accuracy-confidence relationship in medical MLLMs via medical VQA and proposes a Multi-Strategy Fusion approach to improve confidence calibration.

**Results:** The proposed method improves alignment between elicited confidence and true accuracy across medical VQA settings.

**Why it matters:** Confidence calibration in medical VLMs is critical for safe clinical deployment and intersects the reader's focus on evaluation and vision-language models in medicine.


### 3. [Pixel-Level Residual Diffusion Transformer: Scalable 3D CT Volume Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20112)
**Authors:** Zhenkai Zhang, Markus Hiller, Krista A. Ehinger, Tom Drummond  
**arXiv:** `2606.20112`  
**Tags:** 3D CT Generation, Diffusion Transformers, Medical Image Synthesis

**TL;DR:** PRDiT is a pixel-level residual diffusion transformer that generates high-resolution 3D CT volumes directly at voxel level.

**Problem:** Generating high-resolution, fine-detail 3D CT volumes is computationally demanding and optimization-hard for existing generative models.

**Method:** The Pixel-Level Residual Diffusion Transformer (PRDiT) synthesizes 3D medical volumes directly at voxel level using a two-stage residual diffusion design, avoiding latent-space compression that loses detail.

**Results:** PRDiT scalably produces high-quality 3D CT volumes with fine anatomical detail directly in voxel space.

**Why it matters:** Relevant to medical imaging generation and data augmentation for radiology, an area adjacent to the reader's work on chest/CT imaging and generative models.


## Top General AI Papers (Full Summary)

### 1. [Spectral Query-Key Product Weight Steering for Training-Free VLM Hallucination Mitigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20419)
**Authors:** Karn Tiwari, Varnith Chordia, Prathosh A P  
**arXiv:** `2606.20419`  
**Tags:** Vision-Language Models, Hallucination Mitigation, Attention Steering

**TL;DR:** QK Product Steering is a training-free, zero-inference-cost weight edit that reduces object hallucination in VLMs by editing the per-head query-key product.

**Problem:** VLMs frequently generate fluent but visually unsupported descriptions, especially mentioning objects absent from the image.

**Method:** The method directly edits the query-key product operator that produces pre-softmax attention logits, suppressing components linked to hallucination. It is data-free, training-free, and adds zero inference cost.

**Results:** QK Product Steering reduces object hallucination without retraining or runtime overhead.

**Why it matters:** Hallucination control in CLIP-style and generative VLMs is highly relevant to the reader's vision-language foundation model interests, including for trustworthy report generation.


### 2. [The FID Lottery: Quantifying Hidden Randomness in Generative-Model Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20536)
**Authors:** Nicolas Dufour, Alexei A. Efros, Patrick Pérez  
**arXiv:** `2606.20536`  
**Tags:** Generative Model Evaluation, FID, Reproducibility

**TL;DR:** A study treating FID as a random variable over training and sampling seeds, quantifying hidden variance in generative model evaluation.

**Problem:** FID is the de facto image-generation metric, yet papers report a single number from one model and one seed, obscuring its reproducibility.

**Method:** The authors measure FID variance across a two-axis panel of training seeds and generation seeds, directly quantifying how much the reported number fluctuates under retraining and resampling.

**Results:** FID exhibits substantial seed-dependent variance, undermining single-number comparisons between generative models.

**Why it matters:** Connects to the reader's interest in rigorous evaluation metrics for generative models, with direct implications for fair comparison in generative radiology too.


### 3. [Variable-Length Tokenization via Learnable Global Merging for Diffusion Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20076)
**Authors:** Dong Hoon Lee, Seunghoon Hong  
**arXiv:** `2606.20076`  
**Tags:** Tokenization, Diffusion Transformers, Adaptive Compression

**TL;DR:** A variable-length tokenizer for latent diffusion that uses learnable global merging to adaptively balance generation quality and compute.

**Problem:** Latent diffusion models are constrained by a tokenizer's fixed compression ratio, and conventional variable-length tokenizers degrade quality by truncating ordered tokens.

**Method:** The proposed tokenizer varies token counts via a learnable global merging mechanism rather than truncation, allowing diffusion transformers to flexibly trade quality against compute.

**Results:** The approach achieves adaptive compression with better quality-compute tradeoffs than fixed-ratio or truncation-based tokenizers.

**Why it matters:** Tokenizer and foundation-model architecture advances are broadly relevant to the reader's interest in efficient multimodal and generative architectures.
